# Summary
Documentation, Photogrammetry, and 3D Model of the Ressel Cave in Lot, France.

# Background
The Emergence du Ressel is a well known cave but not many divers every expierience the deeper and farther sections of the cave. Additionally navigation in the far sections is still challenging on the first tries due to time constraints and gas logistics. This project aims to bridge that gap through high-resolution digital preservation.

Safety & Navigation: Traditional line surveys often lack the spatial context of wall proximity and verticality. A 3D model provides a superior "pre-dive" visualization tool, helping divers understand the geometry of jumps, circuits and  junctions.

Gear Testing: The scale of the Ressel is allows for a good test of the photogrammetry equipment and the process for large models.

# Goals
System Stress Test: Test the "big" photogrammetry rig with multiple cameras and lights in a high-demand, deep-cave environment.

Testing Photogrammetry Pipeline: Test of the Photogrammetry Pipeline with big projects and reference markers for automated mapping and spatial referencing.

Entrance Documentation: High-detail capture of the cave entrance and initial transition zone.

The Shallow Section: Documentation to 24m, including both jumps, the "secondary" line, the bell, and sidemount-specific passages.

The Shaft: Detailed capture from 20m to 50m, focusing on the intricate narrow sections on the right-hand side.

The Deep Circuit: Documentation of the deep conduit leading up to the "deep circuit" loop.

Section Modells: Creation of individual 3D models for each major cave segment.

Full Model: Integration of all data into a singular, comprehensive 3D digital twin of the Ressel.

Publishing: Publishing of the Data for public access
